I joined a narrative-focused indie game studio as a UI/UX Designer, quickly expanding my role to touch nearly every aspect of the player experience. The small team environment meant wearing multiple hats and learning rapidly across disciplines.

My initial focus was designing intuitive, genre-appropriate user interfaces that felt natural to players. I approached each screen as part of the larger player journey, ensuring menus and HUDs enhanced rather than interrupted the experience.

As I grew into the role, I contributed to core game design including narrative pacing, progression systems, and unlock mechanics. I led playtesting sessions for every build, organizing feedback groups and translating player insights into actionable design improvements. This iterative process helped us refine the game's feel and flow.

I also created in-game assets, working in pixel art and sourcing textures to bring the world to life. Beyond the game itself, I designed the studio's website and developed a cohesive brand identity that captured the studio's narrative-driven approach and indie spirit.
